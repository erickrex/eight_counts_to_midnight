## Instructions

Your Player Journey Map traces the first 15 minutes of the experience you're designing - moment to moment, not system by system. Aim for 1-2 pages. This is one of four required artifacts, alongside your Game Design Document, Visual Concept Package, and Production Plan.

**The 15 minutes is a design lens, not a session length.** Once your game is live, how long a player stays and how often they return is a combination of their choice and how well your design earns it - someone might play for two minutes or twenty. What you control is the experience you design into those first 15 minutes, so design for retention without assuming how long anyone plays.

**Use whatever visual format tells the story best.** You do not have to use the table below. A flowchart, an annotated timeline, storyboard panels, screen mockups with notes, or a clear hand-drawn sketch all work. Mix text and images freely. **The one thing to avoid is a wall of paragraphs - this artifact should be visual.**

Whatever format you choose, map the first 15 minutes across time. Pay special attention to the first 30 seconds - the first impression decides whether they keep going.

_What each row means (a guide, not a script):_

- **What the player sees & does** - the concrete on-screen moment: the screen, the action, the feedback they get.
- **Decision points** - where the player makes a meaningful choice.
- **Difficulty / challenge** - how the challenge ramps, where it spikes, where the player gets to breathe.
- **Emotional beat** - what the player feels in that moment (curiosity, tension, relief, mastery, the "one more round" pull).
- **Natural stop point & return hook** - where a player would comfortably pause, and what pulls them back (a cliffhanger, a timer, an almost-finished goal, a daily reward). This is where session boundaries naturally fall.

**Before you submit:** Check your map against your Game Design Document. The systems your GDD describes should show up here as real moments, and if your GDD promises deep progression, your first 15 minutes should hint at it rather than show a flat experience. Your four artifacts should feel like one game.
