## Instructions

This document is the backbone of your competition submission. It tells judges how you think about game design: what you're designing, why it works, and how it would come together. A strong design document demonstrates genuine design thinking, not just a genre description.

- **Aim for 5-7 pages total.** Suggested lengths per section are guidelines, not hard limits.
- Every section should have enough depth that a reader understands not just what your game does, but **why your design choices make it compelling.**
- This is **one of four required artifacts.** You will also submit a Player Journey Map, Visual Concept Package, and Production Plan. See the competition site for full submission requirements.

For genre expectations and what makes a strong submission, see the [Design Guidance](https://mhcp-game-design.devpost.com/) page on the competition site.

---

## 1. Concept (~0.5-1 page)

What is your game? What is the player trying to accomplish, and what core mechanics do they use to get there?

Paint a clear picture: someone reading this paragraph should be able to visualize what playing your game looks and feels like.

---

## 2. Target Player (~0.5 page)

Who is this game for? What type of person, what play style, what context?

Be specific: *"a casual player looking for 10-minute sessions during a commute"* is more useful than *"anyone who likes games."*

What about your design makes it right for this player?

---

## 3. Core Loop (~0.5-1 page)

What does the player do repeatedly, and why is it satisfying?

Describe the core mechanic in enough detail that someone could understand the moment-to-moment experience:
- What creates the rhythm?
- What makes one cycle different from the next?

This is the engine of your game: show us that it runs.

---

## 4. Progression, Depth & Retention (~1-2 pages)

How does the game evolve over time? What systems keep a player engaged across multiple sessions?

Describe how complexity layers in:
- What's available in **minute 1** vs. **minute 15** vs. **the third session**?

What brings a player back?
- Daily hooks
- Unlocks
- Social elements
- Emergent gameplay
- Narrative beats
- Escalating challenge

Show us the long game, not just the first impression. Focus here on the systems and why they retain — save the moment-to-moment first session for your Player Journey Map.

---

## 5. What Makes It Fun (~0.5-1 page)

Why play twice? What's the hook: the tension, the surprise, the "one more round" feeling?

This is your chance to articulate the **emotional core** of your game. What feeling are you designing for, and how does your design deliver it?

---

## 6. If I Had More Time (~0.5-1 page)

What would you add in a second version? What features or systems did you scope out, and why?

This section shows judges you can think beyond the current design: what's the vision for where this game could go?
