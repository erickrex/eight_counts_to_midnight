## Instructions

This is a design competition. Your production plan shows judges that your game is buildable, that you've thought through how it would come to life, not just what it would be. A realistic 1-page plan is more convincing than an ambitious 3-page plan that tries to cover everything. This is one of four required artifacts, alongside your Game Design Document, Player Journey Map, and Visual Concept Package.

Aim for **1-3 pages.**

## 1. MVP Definition

_What is the minimum version of your game that delivers the core loop? Name exactly which systems from your Game Design Document make the cut and which don't. Include the minimum content your systems need to function - how many resource types, enemy variations, upgrade tiers, levels or waves. "Build the crafting system" isn't specific enough._

_"Crafting with 4 recipes using 3 materials, expandable to 12 recipes in Phase 2" is._

_Example framing: "The MVP is [core mechanic] + [one progression system] + [minimum content: X resource types, Y enemy types, Z levels/waves]. Cut from MVP: [feature], [feature], [feature] - these layer on after the core is validated."_

## 3. Testing & Validation

_Describe your first-playable milestone: when Phase 1 is done, what does the first 2 minutes of your game look and feel like? A player picks it up - what do they see, what do they do, and what tells you it's working?_

_Then:_

- [ ] _What player behavior signals "this is fun"? Be specific: "players replay voluntarily" tells you more than "positive feedback."_
- [ ] _What would make you change direction? What's the signal that the core loop isn't landing, and what would you adjust first?_

## Before you submit

Compare your production plan to your Game Design Document. If your GDD promises six systems but your plan only scopes for two, either the design needs trimming or the plan needs expanding. Your "If I Had More Time" section in the GDD and your "Cut from MVP" items here should tell the same story.
