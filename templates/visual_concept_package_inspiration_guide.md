# Meta Horizon Creator Competition: Game Design

## Visual Concept Package - Inspiration Guide

**Game Title:** ____________________

_Your Visual Concept Package communicates your game's look, feel, and visual identity (up to 10 pages, PDF). This is one of four required artifacts, alongside your Game Design Document, Player Journey Map, and Production Plan. Include at least two anchors so judges can compare submissions: one gameplay screen mockup and one color/mood reference. Beyond those, the format is open - it's your creative direction, presented however you think works best. This guide offers ideas for what you might include._

## What you could include

Pick and combine whatever tells the visual story of your game:

- [ ] **Color palette & mood references** - defined colors with 2-3 reference images that set the feeling. Intentional choices ("warm tones for safe zones, cold for threats") read stronger than arbitrary colors.
- [ ] **Gameplay screen mockups** - what the player actually sees mid-game. HUD placement, interactive elements, how the player reads the game state at a glance. Design for mobile.
- [ ] **UI wireframes** - how the player navigates key interactions (crafting, building, upgrading, wave setup). Labeled elements flow between states.
- [ ] **Character or environment concepts** - visuals that establish who or where. The look and feel of the world the player inhabits.
- [ ] **Mood boards** - visual tone, atmosphere, lighting direction, textural references.
- [ ] **World overview or map concept** - the spatial structure of your game, if applicable.
- [ ] **Key moment panels** - 2-3 scenes showing critical gameplay moments (first encounter, a turning point, a reward).
- [ ] **Style notes** - brief explanation of your art direction choices and what inspired them.

There's no minimum number of items. A focused 3-page package with a clear point of view can score higher than a sprawling 10-page collection. What matters is that it all feels like the same game.

---

## Keep in mind

- [ ] **Coherence over polish.** A rough but consistent direction scores higher than a scattershot of beautiful images.
- [ ] **UI is design.** Where you put information on screen affects how the game plays.
- [ ] **Match your other artifacts.** If your GDD describes a tense survival game, your visuals should feel tense. If your Player Journey Map builds to a dramatic nightfall sequence, show what that looks like.
- [ ] **Design for mobile.** Most Horizon games are played on a phone - design for a mobile screen, portrait or landscape, whatever suits your game.
- [ ] **Original work.** For your final entry submission, all content must be original or use assets that you have rights to. Screenshots from other games are likely copyrighted and shouldn't be included in your submission. Instead, reference those games by name in your documentation, and use original concept art or mockups to convey your visual direction.
