# Meta Horizon Creator Competition: Game Design

## Game Design Document

Author: Erick Alberto Rea

**Game Title:** Eight Counts to Midnight  
**Genre:** Simulation & Management  
**Platform:** Mobile, iOS and Android  
**Orientation:** Portrait  
**Session Target:** 5-10 minutes  

---

## 1. Concept

**Eight Counts to Midnight** is a gothic romance dance-life sim. The player is a mortal Bachata follower who trains by day, styles herself for the ballroom, restores Crimson Manor, and dances at night with Alistair, a cursed vampire lord.

This is **Simulation & Management first**. The player manages time, stats, cards, wardrobe, rooms, and Bond; the dance is where those choices pay off. Each night is shaped by the player's day choices:

| Day Choice | Night Impact |
|---|---|
| Train Technique | Better starting resources and cleaner footwork. |
| Rehearse Cards | Stronger choreography options. |
| Style the Follower | Outfit stats change dance builds. |
| Improve the Manor | Better rooms unlock better preparation. |
| Spend Time with Alistair | Bond makes his lead easier to read. |

The signature hook is simple:

**The first game where deck-construction IS choreography.**

Players build short 8-beat Bachata phrases from cards. Footwork and Transition cards fill the count. Styling and Support cards add expression, safety, romance, and score. When the phrase locks, the UI fades and the cards become a cinematic partner dance.

The core fantasy is growth through rhythm and trust. The player starts as a careful outsider in a haunted ballroom. Over time, she becomes a confident partner who can meet Alistair's lead, restore the manor, and bring warmth back to the castle.

---

## 2. Target Player

The primary player is a mobile romance, fashion, and cozy-progression player, especially women 18-35, who wants short sessions and visible growth. She enjoys character bonding, style choices, and a clear daily routine.

The secondary player likes light strategy and deck construction. The Bachata phrase system gives that player timing, stance, Flow, and Frame puzzles inside a romantic dance fantasy.

| Motivation | How the Game Serves It |
|---|---|
| Romance and connection | Alistair is the love interest and the lead pattern the player learns to read. |
| Style with purpose | Outfits change appearance and performance stats. |
| Tactical expression | Each 8-beat phrase turns card choices into choreography. |

---

## 3. Core Loop

```text
Prepare by day -> Dance by night -> Grow at dawn -> Return stronger tomorrow
```

### Day: Prepare

Each day gives the player a small number of preparation choices. The player might train Technique, rehearse cards, style the follower, spend time with Alistair, or improve a manor space. The limit creates clear tradeoffs. A player preparing for a demanding lead may train Frame or equip gloves. A player chasing style rewards may rehearse a Close-stance phrase or wear a gown that lowers Styling costs.

### Night: Dance

At night, the player performs short 8-beat phrases. Each phrase asks the player to:

- Fill exactly 8 Beats.
- Stay in legal stances. The MVP proves Open and Close; the full game adds Wrap and Shadow.
- Generate Flow through movement.
- Spend Flow on Styling or Support.
- Build Frame for demanding leads.
- Preserve Poise.
- Match Alistair's lead for Connection rewards.

The result is visible right away. Clean choices create a smooth dance replay and a stronger camera moment. Weak choices create a Stumble animation, lower score, and a clear reason to prepare better tomorrow.

### Dawn: Grow

Dawn turns the performance into lasting progress. The player earns Lumen Essence, Bond, card materials, wardrobe materials, and facility progress. These rewards improve future day choices and future dances.

| Loop Step | Player Question | Payoff |
|---|---|---|
| Day | What should I prepare? | Strategic planning. |
| Night | Can I build a clean phrase? | Tactical dance payoff. |
| Dawn | What changed because I danced? | Visible growth and tomorrow's hook. |

## 4. Core Systems Snapshot

| System | Player Manages | Dance Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Day actions | Limited preparation choices. | Sets the resources and tools available at night. |
| Dance cards | Footwork, transitions, styling, and support. | Builds the exact 8-beat phrase. |
| Resources | Beats, Flow, Frame, Poise, Bond, and Lumen Essence. | Controls legality, safety, score, and growth. |
| Stances | Open, Close, Wrap, and Shadow. | Changes which cards, leads, and camera moments are possible. |
| Wardrobe | Shoes, gloves, gowns, jewelry, hair, and perfume. | Adds stats and helps define a dance build. |
| Manor rooms | Ballroom, Mirror Hall, Wardrobe Atelier, and Music Room. | Unlocks better training, style, music, and preparation. |
| Bond | Dialogue, lead study, and matched dances. | Makes Alistair's lead easier to read and unlocks deeper choreography. |

The systems stay connected through one question: **what did the player prepare, and how does it change the next dance?**

## 5. Progression, Depth & Retention

The player grows through connected management layers. Each layer changes both the fantasy and the next dance.

| Layer | Managed Through | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Skills | Technique, Musicality, Frame, Styling, Confidence, Connection. | Better resources, safer leads, stronger rewards. |
| Cards | Rehearsal, upgrades, and deck choices. | More phrase options and cleaner combos. |
| Wardrobe | Gloves, shoes, gowns, jewelry, hairstyles, perfume. | Style becomes both visual identity and build strategy. |
| Manor | Ballroom, Mirror Hall, Wardrobe Atelier, Music Room. | Rooms unlock training, music, styling, and better preparation. |
| Bond | Dialogue, lead study, and matched dances. | Alistair's lead becomes clearer and more intimate. |

### Dance-Card Depth

The card system is built around readable dance verbs:

| Card Role | Function |
|---|---|
| Footwork | Base movement, points, and Flow generation. |
| Transition | Stance changes that unlock the next move. |
| Styling | Visual flair, Bond accents, and score bonuses. |
| Support | Frame, Poise protection, and safer lead response. |

Cards and fantasy use the same language. **Step Into Close** changes the partner position and answers a Close Invitation. **Eye Contact** adds Bond when the player matches Alistair's lead. The system feels like dance from rule to replay.

### Shadow Stance

**Shadow** is the vampire-flavor stance. Open and Close teach readable partner dancing. Shadow unlocks through Bond and lets the follower move inside Alistair's supernatural silhouette. It gives high Lumen and unique vampire choreography, but asks for trust and enough Frame. Shadow ties romance, vampire fantasy, and stance management into one rule.

### Example Phrase

```text
Alistair's Lead: Close Invitation
Goal: Enter Close before Beat 5

Start Stance: Open

Bar A, Beats 1-4:
Foil Basic Step
Open -> Open
Generates 2 Flow

Bar B, Beats 5-6:
Step Into Close
Open -> Close
Adds 10 Frame

Bar B, Beats 7-8:
Close Sway
Close -> Close

Modifier:
Eye Contact attaches to Close Sway
Costs 1 Flow

Result:
8 Beats complete, lead matched, Perfect Connection x2.
The replay shows the player stepping into Alistair's frame as the camera tightens on Beat 8.
```

### Retention

The return hook is clear: tomorrow the player can dance with more style, trust, and control.

| Moment | Player Learns | Next Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Minute 1 | The manor responds to dance. | Finish the first 8-count. |
| Minute 15 | Preparation affects the dance. | Train for a cleaner partner phrase. |
| Third session | Builds change outcomes. | Try wardrobe, Bond, and card upgrades. |
| Later play | The player has a dance identity. | Master advanced leads and Shadow phrases. |

---

### First Three Sessions

| Session | Player Does | What Changes |
|---|---|---|
| Session 1 | Completes a Day 1 solo phrase and cracks Alistair's stone shell. | Learns the 8-beat rule and sees the manor respond to dance. |
| Session 2 | Trains Technique, rehearses cards, answers Close Invitation with Step Into Close. | Sees day preparation create a Perfect Connection. |
| Session 3 | Chooses between a wardrobe/stat boost, a card upgrade, or Bond time before dancing again. | Learns that different builds create different replays and rewards. |

This early arc proves the full loop in a small space: solo confidence, first partner trust, then player choice.

## 6. What Makes It Fun

The emotional core is **becoming worthy of the dance**. The player grows through rhythm, trust, posture, and style.

- **Preparation pays off.** Training, rehearsal, Bond, and outfits create better dance results.
- **Small puzzles feel expressive.** An 8-beat phrase is easy to read, but still gives room for style, timing, stance, and support.
- **Romance is mechanical.** Alistair's lead is a character signal and a gameplay challenge. As Bond grows, the player reads him earlier and earns more intimate choreography.

The "one more round" feeling comes from simple goals:

- Land the lead cleanly.
- Train Frame to support the dip.
- Finish an outfit for a better Close-stance build.
- Reach the next Bond level to see a new invitation.

The design is innovative because **deck-construction is choreography**. The player manages confidence, technique, wardrobe, and intimacy, then proves those choices through a danced phrase.

---

## 7. If I Had More Time

The first version should prove the daily preparation loop, the 8-beat phrase builder, persistent growth, wardrobe function, and one strong relationship arc. Later versions can expand without changing that base.

| Addition | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| More songs and rhythm profiles | Gives Musicality more value. |
| More manor facilities | Adds new training and style paths. |
| Tornado Camera | A dramatic orbiting reward camera that triggers on high-multiplier phrases, stance chains, and supported dips, turning the best moments into cinematic payoffs. |
| Levitation and anti-gravity moves | Supernatural Bachata moves unlocked through Shadow and high Bond, where Alistair's power lifts the follower off the floor for partner shapes no mortal dance allows, [for example Anita and Varea moves](https://youtu.be/Gp0f_zEXzfw?si=iDEyMX8LHO9QEsvJ&t=214). |
| Seasonal dance events | Creates themed goals around costumes, songs, and leads. |
| Deeper wardrobe sets | Builds stronger visual identity and play styles. |
| Social showcase features | Lets players share outfits, phrase replays, and manor snapshots. |
| Advanced dance vocabulary | Adds skill after the 8-beat system is clear. |
| Optional romance routes | Expands the character layer after Alistair's loop works. |

The first build stays focused: one partner, one manor, one daily loop, and one clear dance-card engine.
